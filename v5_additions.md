## Addition 1 — Shively et al. 2025 (disease-context + reference)

Source read: Shively et al. 2025, *Forest Pathology* 55:e70013. Key facts:
- This study's site = Shively's Coastal Plain site **CH** (Chickahominy WMA, VA DWR).
- Disease driven by temperature (negative) + northness (positive); best model
  `AUDPC ~ Treatment × AvgTemp + Treatment × Northness`. AUDPC ~40% lower across
  the warm end of their site range; "disease … effectively mitigated at warmer sites."
- MAT 15.7 °C here ≈ warm extreme of their 9.6–15.8 °C range (zone 8a) → this was a
  **comparatively low-disease site**. (Contradicts the earlier "soil stress → more
  disease" idea, which is dropped.)
